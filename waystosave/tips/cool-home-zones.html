Cool your home in zones

Zoning your central air conditioner keeps air conditioning flowing to rooms you occupy the most while preventing cool air from being wasted in unoccupied rooms.

Overall electricity demand tends to be highest on days with the greatest air conditioning demand, sometimes causing the electricity grid to become stressed. Zone cooling will help you cut your home's energy demand and help maintain a reliable.

How it works A zoned AC has a series of dampers and controls installed in the ducts to prevent air from flowing to unoccupied areas. By contrast, standard, non-zoned duct systems direct cool air to all areas of your home whenever the AC system is operating.

Things to think about

  • If you're remodeling or adding on to your home, zone cooling is a great option to consider.
  • If you have a two-story home, zoned AC is a great feature to consider adding, even if you are not remodeling.

For large households You can create multiple zones in your home and control them separately.
