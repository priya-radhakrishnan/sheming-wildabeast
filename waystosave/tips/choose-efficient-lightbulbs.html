Choose efficient lightbulbs

Why? Inefficient incandescent bulbs, despite their low initial price tag, are costly to run and replace in the long term. Compact fluorescent light bulbs (CFLs) use about 75% less energy and last about 10 times longer, saving you money on energy bills and lighting costs. Halogen light bulbs and light-emitting diodes (LEDs) also provide high-quality light while using less energy than inefficient incandescent bulbs.

What to look for:

  • Find a CFL bulb with about one-quarter the wattage of your old incandescent bulb — CFL bulbs require less power to produce the same amount of light. Most packaging will list equivalent wattage, so you can match the new bulb to what you formerly used.
  • CFL bulbs come in different colors, light intensities and shapes to accommodate most lighting fixtures. Some stores sell light fixtures specially designed to work effectively with specific CFL bulb applications like dimmers, timers or motion sensors.
  • Choose CFL bulbs with the ENERGY STAR® label for guaranteed efficiency and savings.
  • You can find CFL bulbs at hardware, grocery, drug and specialty lighting stores.

Things to think about: First, install CFL bulbs in the lights that you use most often, like those in the kitchen, family room or outside. Consider replacing all your bulbs if you can. If you’re planning on using CFL bulbs outside, choose a lamp with cold weather ballast. CFL bulbs need to be specially recycled when they burn out.

For more facts about CFLs and mercury, including what you should do if a CFL breaks in your home, visit www.energystar.gov/mercury to get EPA recommended guidelines for proper disposal.
